<p>Scotland has a long established base of&nbsp;<strong>hydro</strong>&nbsp;capacity; in <strong>2000</strong>, installed <strong>capacity</strong> was <strong>1.32 GW</strong>.&nbsp; By <strong>September 2021</strong>, this grew by 25% to&nbsp;<strong>1.66 GW</strong>, the growth coming from small scale installations of less than 5 MW.&nbsp; The number of <strong>sites</strong> generating electricity via hydro rose from <strong>163</strong> in <strong>2003</strong> to <strong>763</strong>&nbsp;in <strong>2020</strong>.&nbsp;</p>
<p>In <strong>2020</strong>, Scotland generated almost <strong>6.2 TWh </strong>via hydro, the equivalent of powering more than <strong>half&nbsp;</strong>of&nbsp;<strong>Scotland&rsquo;s homes&nbsp;</strong>for a year.&nbsp;</p>
