<p>It is possible to look at the&nbsp;<strong>fuel mix</strong>&nbsp;which makes up&nbsp;<strong>regional consumption</strong>&nbsp;via the National Grid&rsquo;s&nbsp;<a href="https://carbonintensity.org.uk/">Carbon Intensity website</a>.</p>
<p>It shows that in the twelve months ending&nbsp;<strong>September 2021,</strong>&nbsp;<strong>84.5%</strong>&nbsp;of the electricity that&nbsp;<strong>Scotland</strong>&nbsp;<strong>consumed</strong>&nbsp;came from&nbsp;<strong>low carbon</strong> sources,&nbsp;<strong>up</strong>&nbsp;from<strong> 84.4%</strong>&nbsp;in the twelve months ending&nbsp;<strong>September 2020</strong>.&nbsp; Almost half (<strong>49.4%</strong>) of the electricity Scotland consumes is from&nbsp;<strong>renewable</strong> sources, almost two-fifths (<strong>38.0%</strong>) from&nbsp;<strong>wind</strong>.</p>
<p>As with generation,&nbsp;<strong>consumption</strong>&nbsp;from electricity from&nbsp;<strong>low carbon</strong> (<strong>49.0%</strong>) and&nbsp;<strong>renewable</strong>&nbsp;(<strong>32.2%</strong>) sources are substantially&nbsp;<strong>lower</strong>&nbsp;across&nbsp;<strong>England and Wales</strong>.</p>
