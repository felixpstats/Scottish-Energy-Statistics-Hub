<p>In the last ten years, the number of electricity and gas suppliers in the Scottish market increased substantially &ndash; as of <strong>2010</strong> Scottish consumers had the choice of <strong>12</strong> suppliers for <strong>electricity or gas</strong>.&nbsp; This rose to <strong>70</strong> active suppliers in the domestic gas and electricity retail markets by <strong>June 2018</strong>, however it dropped to <strong>55</strong>&nbsp;in <strong>September 2020</strong> due to suppliers exiting the market.&nbsp; Regardless, consumers have a much greater choice of supplier in the last decade.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Despite the increased choice the&nbsp;<strong>largest six suppliers</strong>&nbsp;&ndash; Scottish Power, SSE, British Gas, E.ON, npower and EDF &ndash; still collectively dominate the market.&nbsp; They supply electricity and gas to&nbsp;<strong>80.1%</strong>&nbsp;in&nbsp;<strong>North Scotland</strong>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<strong>74.6%</strong>&nbsp;in&nbsp;<strong>South Scotland</strong>.&nbsp;&nbsp; Based on market shares, Ofgem classify other suppliers into medium and small &ndash; South Scotland have a higher share of both than North Scotland.</p>
