<p><strong>Electricity consumption declined </strong>steadily in Scotland compared to the 2005-07 baseline, down by <strong>22.5%</strong> in <strong>2020</strong>. The difference in electricity used in 2005-07 and 2020 (6,591 GWh) the equivalent of more than half of the electricity used by Scottish households in 2020. This drop in electricity consumption is more pronounced in the <strong>non-domestic</strong> sector (<strong>26.0%</strong>) than the <strong>domestic</strong> sector (<strong>17.6%</strong>).</p>
<p>Electricity consumption is at its lowest annual level in Scotland in <strong>2020</strong>, <strong>down 5.7%</strong> on <strong>2019</strong> to <strong>22.7 TWh</strong>.&nbsp; This decrease is concentrated in the <strong>non-domestic sector</strong>, where consumption <strong>decreased </strong>by <strong>12.2%</strong>, whereas it <strong>increased</strong> by<strong> 4.2%</strong> in the <strong>domestic</strong> sector. This is likely a result of coronavirus restrictions throughout the year</p>
<p><strong>Average</strong> <strong>electricity consumption</strong> per <strong>household</strong> in Scotland is <strong>4,001 kWh</strong>, <strong>down</strong> by <strong>24.5%</strong> on <strong>2005-07</strong>. Average electricity consumption by household is significantly higher in the islands, because households are predominantly off the gas grid and therefore more likely to use electricity to heat their homes.</p>
