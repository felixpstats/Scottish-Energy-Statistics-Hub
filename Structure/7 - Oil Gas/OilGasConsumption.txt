<p>Even with the growth of renewables in recent years, oil and gas continue to make up most of Scotland&rsquo;s energy consumption. In 2019:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Oil and petroleum </strong>make up <strong>43.7%</strong> of all Scottish consumption and <strong>gas </strong>makes up a third (<strong>32.8%</strong>). Just <strong>23.5% </strong>of all Scottish consumption comes from <strong>other fuels</strong>. This means that <strong>oil and gas </strong>make up <strong>76.5% </strong>of <strong>all Scottish consumption, </strong>slightly higher than <strong>73.4% </strong>for the <strong>UK </strong>as a whole<strong>.</strong></li>
<li>In the <strong>domestic </strong>sector in Scotland, <strong>gas </strong>makes up two-thirds of all consumption (<strong>65.4%</strong>).</li>
<li>Oil and gas make up <strong>almost all </strong>of Scotland&rsquo;s <strong>transport </strong>consumption, and <strong>89.9% </strong>of its <strong>heat </strong>demand.</li>
</ul>
