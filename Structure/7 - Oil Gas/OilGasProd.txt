<p>In&nbsp;<strong>2017</strong>, Scotland (including Scottish adjacent waters) is estimated to have&nbsp;<strong>produced 48.7&nbsp;</strong>million tonnes of oil equivalent (<strong>mtoe</strong>) of crude&nbsp;<strong>oil&nbsp;</strong>and natural gas liquids (NGLs) (equivalent to&nbsp;<strong>567 TWh</strong>).&nbsp;&nbsp;While this is approximately a third of what it was in 1999, it is an&nbsp;<strong>increase&nbsp;</strong>of almost 8 mtoe since&nbsp;<strong>2014</strong>.&nbsp;&nbsp;<strong>Scotland&nbsp;</strong>accounts for&nbsp;<strong>95.7%&nbsp;</strong>of total&nbsp;<strong>UK</strong>&nbsp;crude oil and NGLs&nbsp;<strong>production</strong>.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p>
<p>Scotland produced&nbsp;<strong>24.9 mtoe&nbsp;</strong>of natural&nbsp;<strong>gas&nbsp;</strong>in&nbsp;<strong>2017&nbsp;</strong>(equivalent to&nbsp;<strong>290 TWh</strong>), accounting for&nbsp;<strong>62.3%&nbsp;</strong>of total&nbsp;<strong>UK&nbsp;</strong>gas production.&nbsp;&nbsp;This is the equivalent of&nbsp;<strong>six times Scotland&rsquo;s gas consumption</strong>.</p>
