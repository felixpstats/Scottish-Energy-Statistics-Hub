<p>The Scottish electricity demand data is available at a settlement period, or half-hourly level.&nbsp; The first chart shows half-hourly demand for the most recent week compared to the same period in 2020 (which is before national lockdown was imposed) and 2019.&nbsp; The second shows the average demand of each half hour period of the year so far, compared to the same period in the two years previous.</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Peak demand</strong> during weekdays was similar, - <strong>4,715 MW</strong> in <strong>2021</strong> compared to <strong>5,037 MW</strong> in the same week in <strong>2019, and 4,188 MW </strong>in <strong>2020</strong>.</li>
<li>Weekend demand shows a different picture. Whilst there is usually reduced demand on weekends, the drop in average weekend demand so far is more pronounced in <strong>2021</strong> compared to the two years previous.</li>
</ul>
